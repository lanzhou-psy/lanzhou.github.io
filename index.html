I am currently a PhD student in Psychiatry from University Medical Center of Groningen (UMCG).

As a prospective neuropsychologist, I am interested in studying the impact of childhood adversities on mental health. My current research involves machine learning and network analysis to examine the effect of trauma on the neurodevelopment of schizophrenia using MRI data. I am also intereted in the association between childhood trauma and inflammation. Overall, my goal is to advance our understanding of how early life stress contributes to mental illness and improve possible treatment options.

In my Master's research, I used fMRI as the tool to investigate the reward processing and the neural basis of anhedonia in indviduals with schizophrenia and schizotypal trait at East China Normal University and Shanghai New York University.

In my bachelor, I did some small projects about the association between air pollution and health at Shanghai Jiao Tong University.
